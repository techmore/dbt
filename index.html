Start Here

This site is for beginners. It gives you a repeatable routine, simple choices when you do not know what you feel, and a short list of strong DBT resources to keep you focused. This is not a diagnosis or treatment replacement. If you are in crisis, call emergency services or 988 in the US immediately.

What this site gives you

  • A step-by-step 0-to-1 starting protocol
  • Daily guidance that fits in 20–35 minutes
  • Option-based forms when you cannot name feelings clearly
  • A resource tab for reinforcement and review

What to use it with

  • Sleep, food, water, movement
  • Short phone-free practice blocks
  • A coach or therapist, if available
  • One skill at a time, repeated daily

From 0 to 1: the first 14 days

Day 1–2: Stabilize

Only two habits:

  1. Check your body every 3 hours: jaw, shoulders, breathing, hands, stomach, feet
  2. Use the evening selector sheet to pick an emotion, trigger, and response

Body-check means: “Where in my body do I feel tension right now?”

Day 3–5: Build observation

Add one core skill:

  • Mindfulness of current emotions before reacting
  • Say: “I am having the emotion, not being the emotion.”

Day 6–10: Add regulation loop

Add one distress tolerance skill:

  • TIPP (cold water, brief movement, paced breathing)
  • Use on first sign of escalation, then continue the routine

Day 11–14: Build interpersonal capacity

Keep existing habits and add one communication target:

  • Use one DEAR skill sentence during a short real-life interaction
  • Keep interactions brief and specific to one objective

Daily Schedule

Keep it short. Repeat the same structure until it becomes automatic.

Body check

Notice tension, breathing speed, jaw, shoulders, hands, stomach, feet. The goal is to catch stress before it turns into action.

Paced breathing

Slow, steady breathing on a count. A simple version is 4 seconds in, 6 seconds out. Box breathing is another option: 4 in, hold 4, out 4, hold 4.

Activated

Meaning: your body and mind are ramped up, irritated, panicked, flooded, shut down, or close to doing something impulsive.

Self-soothing

Meaning: a non-harmful action that calms your senses or body, such as cold water, a walk, a warm drink, music, texture, or slow breathing.

Morning (7–12 min)

  • 2-minute body check
  • Choose one focus word: calm, steady, clear, patient, or firm
  • 3 minutes of paced breathing (4-6 count cycle)
  • 10-second values statement: “Today I will prioritize one useful action.”

Midday (8–12 min)

  • Use STOP if you notice stress building or you are about to react fast
  • STOP = Stop, Take a step back, Observe, Proceed mindfully
  • Choose one self-soothing action from the list below

Evening (8–15 min)

  • Fill out the selector sheet: emotion, trigger, response
  • Write one alternative response for tomorrow
  • Choose tomorrow’s one-skill focus

DBT Skill Set

DBT is usually taught as four core modules. This page keeps them visible and practical.

Mindfulness

Core goal: increase awareness before action.

  • Observe, Describe, Participate
  • One thing at a time during one task
  • “This is hard, and I can do it in pieces”

Emotion Regulation

Core goal: reduce emotional volatility.

  • Check current emotions for intensity, duration, context
  • Build one opposite action and do it consistently
  • Target sleep and hydration first

Distress Tolerance

Core goal: survive urges without harm.

  • TIPP protocol at first wave of overwhelm
  • Self-soothing by senses: cold, texture, scent
  • Disaster survival plan for impulsive moments

Interpersonal Effectiveness

Core goal: keep your values and boundaries in conversation.

  • DEAR: Describe, Express, Assert, Reinforce
  • One objective, one sentence, one request
  • Short scripts prevent escalation under stress

Chain Analysis

Core goal: understand sequence and build alternatives.

  • Prompt → vulnerability → interpretation → emotion → urge → action → consequence
  • Keep it to 1 chain per difficult moment
  • Use your starter sheet as the “first draft”

Weekly Structure (Not Overwhelming)

Week 1

Use only one skill per day and one daily checkpoint. Keep sleep baseline stable.

Week 2

Add emotional tracking in the evening and one interpersonal target on 2 days.

Week 3

Keep previous habits and add one interpersonal communication script on 3 days.

Week 4+

Repeat with one refinement each week: slower pace, clearer boundaries, better recovery speed.
