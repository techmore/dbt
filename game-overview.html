Veil of Balance: A DBT Journey

A single-player narrative RPG about being pulled into Glimmerfall, an emotional mirror world where skillful presence, compassionate connection, and quiet courage are the path home.

Progression Outline

These are the intended story areas and mechanic ramps. Each region introduces one new DBT idea through play, then folds it into later systems so the player learns by doing.

1. Arrival: The Shattered Verge

A ruined threshold zone where the player wakes up disoriented and learns basic movement, camera, interaction, and the first Observe & Describe moment.

mindfulness orientation first contact

2. Lantern Road

A safer travel route with companion encounters and dialogue choices that teach validation, grounding, and low-pressure trust building.

validation relationship flags guided dialogue

3. Stormglass Marsh

A distressed landscape that changes with mood state. This area introduces distress tolerance, body-based reset actions, and simple environmental hazard pressure.

distress tolerance weather shift tempo control

4. Echo Market

A crowded, socially dense hub where interpersonal effectiveness matters. DEAR MAN choices shape access, prices, and faction reactions.

dear man social choice reputation

5. Hollow Cathedral

A reflective midgame zone focused on emotion regulation, opposite action, and choosing calm under symbolic pressure.

emotion regulation opposite action belief challenge

6. Weaving Arena: Fracture Courts

The first major combat space where emotional battles become tactical weaving fights and the player learns to absorb, stabilize, and redirect force rather than overwhelm it.

combat tutorial absorb resource control

7. Blooming Ruins

A late-game area where acceptance and change are both required. The player sees the world regain life, but only if prior relationships and balance states were maintained.

dialectics endgame pressure hope

8. Mirror Apex

The final ascent where all mechanics recombine. The ending changes based on emotional balance, relationship repair, and whether the player chose avoidance or integration.

final choice multiple endings homecoming

Game Overview

The player arrives in Glimmerfall as an emotionally dysregulated outsider and learns to move through chaos by observing, naming, regulating, and reconnecting. The DBT material is present in the mechanics, not presented as therapy homework.

Tone

  • Dreamlike and melancholy, with flashes of wonder
  • Escaflowne-style fantasy scale, but emotionally intimate
  • Hopeful without flattening pain

Core loop

  • Explore shifting environments
  • Talk, validate, and choose responses
  • Use Weaving skills in metaphorical emotional battles
  • Write reflections in the Inner Diary

Design Pillars

Story first

Every skill should emerge through a dramatic moment, not a tutorial prompt.

Emotional legibility

Visuals, music, and world state should clearly reflect the protagonist’s inner weather.

Dialectic play

Choices should reward both acceptance and change rather than forcing a single “correct” answer.

Replayability

Different starting profiles, relationship paths, and balance states should change the journey.

Asset / Sprite Checklist

Playable character assets

  • 3D or 2.5D model with 3 core materials: dysregulated, steady, balanced
  • Idle, walk, interact, hurt, and mindfulness pose animation
  • Portrait set for dialogue and journal pages
  • Simple UI icon set for mindfulness, distress tolerance, and validation

Companion assets

  • Portraits with neutral, concerned, supportive, and resolved expressions
  • Small dialogue bust sprites or portrait frames
  • Faction or relationship markers for the journal UI

World assets

  • Dreamy ruins, floating stone, mirror ponds, storm fields, and lantern bridges
  • Transition VFX for emotional weather shifts
  • Interactable landmarks for mindfulness and dialogue scenes

Enemy assets

  • Entropy Beast silhouettes with readable elemental variants
  • Attack tell effects and defeat dissolves
  • Boss-scale forms that feel symbolic rather than grotesque

UI assets

  • Dialogue frame
  • Choice buttons
  • Emotion meter or balance ring
  • Inner Diary page styling

Audio assets

  • Ambient overworld loop with airy pads and soft percussion
  • Combat cue layers that intensify without becoming harsh
  • Calm, reflective diary stinger
  • Weather shift transitions for storm, bloom, and hush states

Generation Pipeline

  1. Generate character turnarounds and portraits first.
  2. Create one overworld biome test scene: ruined meadow, storm sky, reflective water.
  3. Make one Entropy Beast and one companion dialogue set.
  4. Build mood-state variants for the protagonist.
  5. Iterate on the UI once the first playable loop feels good.

Important note

This project is DBT-inspired and meant to be emotionally supportive in tone, but it is not therapy and should never present itself as a substitute for professional care.
